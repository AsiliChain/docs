Middlemen capture 60 to 70 percent of the coffee value chain because farmers have no visibility into forward prices, no verified records to negotiate with, and no alternative source of credit. The farmer who needs cash for fertiliser in February will pre-sell their October harvest at a fraction of its worth to whoever is willing to lend. This is the financing mechanism AsiliChain is designed to replace with on-chain collateral and auto-repaying loans.

| Structural Problem | Consequence for Farmers |
| --- | --- |
| Price extraction by intermediaries | Farmers earn ~$0.20/kg. Retail price exceeds $10/kg. Middlemen capture the difference. |
| No supply chain traceability | Quality disputes, rejected shipments, EUDR non-compliance, zero audit trail. |
| Zero access to formal credit | Forced pre-season sales to informal lenders at exploitative rates. |
| Paper-based record-keeping | No verifiable credit history, no negotiating position, no collateral. |
| No smartphone or crypto wallet | Excluded from virtually all existing fintech solutions. |
