7.6 What a Middleman Actually Does

Before describing the solution, it is worth being precise about what AsiliChain is actually displacing. A middleman in the coffee supply chain performs four functions, only two of which create genuine value.

The first is aggregation - collecting small batches from many farmers into export-grade volumes. This is operationally real and cooperatives already perform it. AsiliChain does not displace this.

The second is logistics - arranging transport, warehousing, and customs clearance. Also real. Also not displaced by AsiliChain.

The third is price discovery - knowing what buyers will pay and what sellers will accept, and keeping that information asymmetric so the spread belongs to the intermediary. This is where extraction happens. AsiliChain's Chainlink price feed, on-chain batch metadata, and buyer portal make this information symmetric for the first time. Neither party needs a broker to tell them what Grade A Arabica from Nyeri is worth today.

The fourth is credit provision - advancing cash to farmers before harvest in exchange for the right to buy the harvest at a below-market price. This is the deepest form of extraction. AsiliChain's LendingVault replaces it entirely with collateral-backed lending at governed interest rates.

Displacing the third and fourth functions while leaving the first and second intact is what the committed purchase order model achieves.
