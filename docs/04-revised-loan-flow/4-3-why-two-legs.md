title: 4.3 Why Two Legs Is the Right Design
sidebar_position: 3
---

The two-leg approach is more complex than a single-chain design but it is the correct architecture for three reasons. First, it keeps the farmer experience entirely on Celo rails, which are proven and live. The farmer never interacts with Mantle and never needs to know it exists. Second, it keeps the institutional audit trail entirely on Mantle, where MFI partners and regulatory counterparties have a clean USDC-denominated record of every loan. Third, the cooperative float model means the bridging cost of CCIP is amortised across many disbursements rather than paid per loan, which is economically efficient at cooperative scale.

The float size required is modest. A cooperative disbursing UGX 50 million per month (approximately USD 13,000) needs a cUSD float of roughly one to two weeks of disbursements as a working buffer. This is well within the operational capacity of any UCDA-registered cooperative that is already managing export receipts in the tens of millions of shillings.
