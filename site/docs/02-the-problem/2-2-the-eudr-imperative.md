title: 2.2 The EUDR Imperative
sidebar_position: 2
---

Under Article 4 of EU Regulation 2023/1115, operators placing coffee on the EU market must file a Due Diligence Statement for every shipment, containing GPS polygon coordinates for every plot of land in the supply chain, deforestation-free proof verified against a 2020 baseline, and a declaration of legal production. For cooperatives sourcing from hundreds of smallholder farms, this is structurally impossible with paper records.

As UNDP has documented, risk-averse international buyers may concentrate purchases in producing countries best organised to comply, leaving more vulnerable African producers out in the cold. The EUDR enforcement delay granted in late 2024 does not change the underlying requirement. It gives African producers more time to build the infrastructure that compliance demands. AsiliChain is that infrastructure.
