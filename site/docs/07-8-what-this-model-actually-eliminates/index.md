The committed purchase order model eliminates specifically the third and fourth middleman functions described above - price discovery asymmetry and predatory credit provision - while leaving physical aggregation and logistics untouched.

A cooperative using AsiliChain with a committed European buyer no longer needs an exporter in the middle whose primary value was knowing the buyer's willingness to pay and the farmer's need for cash. The cooperative knows the buyer's committed price. The farmer accesses credit through the `LendingVault` rather than through a pre-sale to an intermediary. The buyer knows exactly what they are buying and has a compliance package ready before the shipment leaves the warehouse.

The exporter who adds genuine value - logistics, customs, physical quality assurance - can still participate. AsiliChain does not prevent this. What it prevents is the exporter who adds no value beyond controlling information from capturing a margin for that non-service.

## Is AsiliChain a Marketplace?

Not in the exchange sense. AsiliChain does not operate a bid-ask matching engine, does not hold inventory, and does not intermediate between unknown buyers and sellers the way a commodity exchange does. It is infrastructure on which direct relationships between cooperatives and buyers can be formalized, enforced, and settled.

The distinction matters for two reasons.

Regulatorily, operating an exchange in Uganda, Kenya, or any of the target markets triggers Capital Markets Authority oversight and potentially commodity exchange licensing requirements. AsiliChain as infrastructure-for-direct-relationships avoids this classification.

Operationally, a full matching exchange requires active market-making, liquidity provision, and dispute resolution at scale - operational complexity that is premature before the protocol has proven itself with committed cooperative and buyer partners.

The committed purchase order model is the first step. If cooperative and buyer adoption reaches the point where a broader matching function creates genuine value - where a cooperative wants to sell to the highest bidder rather than a committed partner - that feature can be added through governance as a Phase 4 or later decision. The infrastructure will already support it. The decision to activate it is a business and regulatory choice, not a technical one.
