title: 7.9 What This Means for the Whitepaper's Value Chain Claim
sidebar_position: 8.9
---

The whitepaper states that middlemen capture 60 to 70 percent of the coffee value chain. That figure needs a mechanism attached to it, not just a number. The mechanism is this: middlemen capture that share because they control price information and credit access simultaneously.

Remove the credit access function with the `LendingVault` and the `GrowingCropToken`. Remove the price information asymmetry with the committed purchase order and the Chainlink price feed visible to both parties. What remains is the legitimate operational margin for physical handling, logistics, and quality assurance - which is a fraction of the current 60 to 70 percent.

The cooperative does not become a commodity broker. The farmer does not negotiate directly with a roaster in Amsterdam. But the farmer knows what their coffee is worth because the committed price is on-chain, and they receive the majority of that price because no information asymmetry justifies a 60 percent extraction between them and the buyer who wants it.
